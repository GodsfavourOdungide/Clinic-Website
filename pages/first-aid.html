Always Remember

The Golden Rules of First Aid

Before acting, these principles guide every correct response in any emergency situation.

1

Stay Calm & Assess

Take a breath. Assess the scene for safety before approaching. A panicked response often worsens outcomes.

2

Call Emergency Services

For serious incidents, call 112 immediately. First aid buys time — professional help is always needed for major emergencies.

3

Do No Further Harm

Only move a patient if they are in immediate danger. Incorrect movement can worsen spinal, neck, or internal injuries.

4

Keep Them Warm & Monitored

Shock is common after injury. Keep the person warm, still, and reassured while you await professional help.

5

Know Your Limits

Providing comfort, controlling bleeding, and basic airway management are your priority — not complex medical procedures.

6

Document & Report

Note the time, what happened, and what you did. This information is critical for paramedics and emergency physicians.

Scene One

Home Emergencies

Common household emergencies and how to respond before professional help arrives.

Moderate

Burns & Scalds

  • 1 Cool burn under running water for 10–20 minutes.
  • 2 Remove jewellery near the burn — unless stuck.
  • 3 Cover loosely with sterile bandage or cling film.
  • 4 Do NOT apply butter, oil, toothpaste, or ice.
HomeLearn more
Urgent

Choking

  • 1 Ask "Are you choking?" — if they can speak, encourage coughing.
  • 2 Give up to 5 back blows between shoulder blades.
  • 3 Perform up to 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich).
  • 4 Alternate until obstruction clears.
HomeLearn more
Urgent

Poisoning

  • 1 Call Poison Control or 112 immediately.
  • 2 Do NOT induce vomiting unless directed.
  • 3 If on skin, flush with cool water for 15–20 minutes.
  • 4 If eye contamination, rinse eye gently for 15 minutes.
HomeLearn more
Manageable

Cuts & Wounds

  • 1 Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth.
  • 2 Elevate the injured area above the heart.
  • 3 Rinse wound gently once bleeding slows.
  • 4 Apply antiseptic cream and cover with bandage.

Scene Two

Office & Workplace Emergencies

Prepare your workplace with the knowledge to act when it matters most.

Critical

Suspected Heart Attack

  • 1 Call 112 immediately — do not drive them yourself.
  • 2 Help them sit in a comfortable position.
  • 3 Loosen any tight clothing.
  • 4 If available, give 300mg aspirin to chew slowly.
OfficeLearn more
Moderate

Fainting / Collapse

  • 1 Lower the person to the ground safely.
  • 2 Lay them flat and elevate legs 30cm.
  • 3 Loosen any tight clothing.
  • 4 Check for breathing every 30 seconds.
OfficeLearn more
Moderate

Chemical Eye Splash

  • 1 Immediately flush eye with running water for 15–20 min.
  • 2 Hold eyelids open, let water flow inner to outer corner.
  • 3 Remove contact lenses — do not rub the eye.
  • 4 Seek emergency ophthalmology care immediately.
OfficeLearn more
Critical

Electric Shock

  • 1 DO NOT touch the person if still in contact with source.
  • 2 Switch off power at the mains.
  • 3 Call 112 immediately once safe.
  • 4 If unconscious but breathing, place in recovery position.

Scene Three

Road Traffic Accidents

Critical steps to take at a road accident before emergency services arrive.

Critical

Securing the Scene

  • 1 Turn on hazard lights, park well clear.
  • 2 Set warning triangles 45m behind.
  • 3 Call 112 immediately — give exact location.
  • 4 Turn off all vehicle engines to reduce fire risk.
RoadLearn more
Critical

Spinal / Neck Injury

  • 1 DO NOT move the person unless in immediate danger.
  • 2 Keep head, neck, and spine aligned.
  • 3 Hold the head gently in position found.
  • 4 Monitor breathing continuously until help arrives.
RoadLearn more
Critical

Severe Bleeding

  • 1 Wear gloves if available. Apply direct pressure.
  • 2 Do not remove embedded objects.
  • 3 Elevate limb above heart level if no fracture.
  • 4 Watch for signs of shock.
RoadLearn more
Moderate

Suspected Fractures

  • 1 Immobilize the area — do not attempt to straighten.
  • 2 Pad around the fracture to prevent movement.
  • 3 Splint using a stiff object.
  • 4 Check circulation every 10 minutes.

Scene Four

Outdoor & Other Emergencies

From heatstroke to allergic reactions — how to handle emergencies wherever you are.

Urgent

Heatstroke

  • 1 Move to cool, shaded area. Call 112 if severe.
  • 2 Remove excess clothing, cool with cool water.
  • 3 Fan the person to aid evaporation.
  • 4 If conscious, give small sips of cool water.
OutdoorLearn more
Critical

Severe Allergic Reaction

  • 1 Call 112 immediately — anaphylaxis can be fatal.
  • 2 Administer epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen).
  • 3 Lay person flat with legs elevated.
  • 4 Administer second EpiPen after 5 minutes if no improvement.
OutdoorLearn more
Critical

Near-Drowning

  • 1 Do not enter water unless trained — extend a rope.
  • 2 Call 112 as soon as person is out of water.
  • 3 Check breathing — if not, begin CPR immediately.
  • 4 Keep warm — remove wet clothing.
OutdoorLearn more
Urgent

Snake Bite

  • 1 Move away from snake, call 112 immediately.
  • 2 Keep bitten area below heart level.
  • 3 Remove tight clothing before swelling.
  • 4 Do NOT cut wound, suck venom, or apply tourniquet.

Know the Signals

Warning Signs That Need Immediate Care

Never wait on these symptoms — call emergency services or attend A&E immediately.

Chest Pain or Pressure

Tightness radiating to arm, jaw, or back.

Call 112 Immediately

Difficulty Breathing

Sudden shortness of breath, wheezing.

Call 112 Immediately

FAST — Stroke Signs

Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech slurring.

Act Within Minutes

Throat Swelling

Rapid throat swelling after eating or stings.

Call 112 Immediately

Head Trauma

Loss of consciousness after head blow.

Seek Emergency Care

Seizures

First-time or lasting more than 5 minutes.

Call 112 Immediately
